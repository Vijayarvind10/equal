About

Neighbors helping neighbors, with structure

NeighborLift Network started as a rapid response toolkit for one apartment block. Now it helps coordinators in dozens of towns launch dependable volunteer circuits in days, not months.

01

Mission and promise

Our mission

To make short-term care practical. We give local teams the tools to run errands, rides, and phone check-ins with dignity, consistency, and transparent safety practices.

Community promise

Residents always choose how they are supported. Volunteers receive clear agreements, background checks, and escalation protocols before a single visit begins.

02

How we work

Start with a circuit plan

Every new team receives a six-week planner with staffing ratios, task checklists, and SMS reminders they can copy into any scheduling app.

Build in safety

We require buddy shifts, coordinator escalation contacts, and consent logs for entry to every home. Incident forms and support numbers are standard.

Track what matters

Volunteer daybooks feed into simple dashboards that show visits completed, upcoming requests, and unresolved concerns so leads can intervene early.

Share what works

Successful circuit templates go back into the library so the next block can launch faster and avoid common pitfalls.

03

Who keeps the network running

Coordinators collective

Volunteer leads from every active circuit meet bi-weekly to review safety logs, tech upgrades, and new resource requests.

Support desk

A small staff team handles the hotline, resource design, and partnerships with clinics, libraries, and housing associations.

04

Values in action

  • Trust through clarity. Every volunteer knows the script, every resident knows the schedule.
  • Safety first. Consent is confirmed before contact, and we log every escalation.
  • Learning loops. We document adjustments and share them openly with other neighborhoods.
