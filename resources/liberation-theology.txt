Liberation Theology in Plain Language

60-SECOND OVERVIEW
Use this primer to connect faith traditions with movements for justice in LALS 65.
Students leave with shared vocabulary and a reflection prompt that links belief to action.

TIMING
60 minutes (lecture plus circle discussion)

MATERIALS
Mini timeline handout with key movements
Story cards drawn from current community examples
Circle prompt script with consent reminders

STEPS
Open with a two minute story showing faith in action and invite silent reflection.
Define liberation theology in three anchors: context, key voices, everyday practices.
Map a historic case study in small groups using the timeline handout.
Circle share: ask what responsibilities show up when faith meets organizing.
Close with a quiet write: one concrete commitment and one question for follow up.

FOLLOW-UP
Collect exit tickets and email a short reading list within 24 hours to deepen context.
